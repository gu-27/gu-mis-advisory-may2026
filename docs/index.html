Context

Looking Ahead

"The restructuring is largely behind us. The next chapter is about identity, positioning, and preparing graduates for a market that is shifting faster than ever."
The Market Reality
  • Tougher entry-level job market for graduates
  • Industry professionals losing roles, increasing competition
  • Tools lowering the barrier to entry and raising the bar for differentiation
"We will not give up the fundamentals. They are why our students last. But we have a real opportunity to equip students with emerging skills that let them stand out — to come in not just credentialed, but ready to add value in ways their more experienced peers may not yet."

Opportunities & Cautions

Opportunities
"Looking five years out, as technology shifts and tools mature, what capabilities should an Information Systems graduate have developed? What separates a graduate who stands out from one who blends in? Think across domains: finance, supply chain, healthcare, tech itself."
Cautions
"What would you caution us against? Where do we risk losing depth if we chase what's trending?"
We are particularly interested in how to build out the Information Systems concentration. That is where we have the most flexibility for emerging coursework.
